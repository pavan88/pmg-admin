Frequently Asked Questions

What is PMG?

ProMaGizmo, or Profit Making Gizmo, is a gadget that helps you earn money. Simple as that. For people in the Adsense or similar business, PMG is a perfect place to hangout and exchange their links that contain the ads in them for others to click. And hence earn money, while also helping fellow marketers earn too. PMG gives the power to the people, coz it is a people driven ecosystem which until now, was controlled by Google alone.

What PMG is not?

PMG is not a traditional traffic exchanging setup. It is not automated.Only humans do whatever is done here. It is targeted at making the clicks happen on your ads in a completely natural manner, so as to protect your adsense or similar business from being banned/blacklisted. PMG is not self-click oriented. And since it is completely driven by us human beings, it is made sure that there are no detectable patterns created while you browse through the ads. This keeps us one step ahead of Google.

How does it work?

They click your ads, you click theirs. Period. But not the way you’re used to. We have strived really hard to make PMG help people to build a symbiotic ecosystem. And it’s no rocket science either. If you are into adsense or something similar, we’re sure you wait for people to click on your advertisements. These clicks help you earn money after all. PMG ensures that all the hour you invested in building that website, content writing, SEO, SMO etc. and getting the ads displayed at your website, do not go in vain. There are mainly three pillars that constitute the PMG. Here's the details.

The Broadcaster's end:

• As a broadcaster, you feed your links that contain the ads in them (we will refer to these as ad links for simplicity's sake), to PMG. Now, you can feed in as many links as you please from the domain that you had mentioned while registering at PMG. But at the backend, PMG's intelligent algorithms generate a unique ID for each link and select a fixed number of links from those submitted by you.

• These links are selected randomly by PMG's advanced algorithms. How many links will be fetched, depends on the user level you're at. For more on user levels, scroll down.

• When PMG is done selecting the ad links at random from those submitted by you, it broadcasts those ad links to other users for them to click on those ad links. One link goes to one user at a time. And this happens on a daily basis.

• This process repeats itself day by dayuntil PMG is done broadcasting all of the ad links submitted by you. The broadcaster receives an intimation from the PMG, telling them when their list of submitted links is about to get exhausted.

The receiver's end:

• The receiver receives the ad links in the form of corresponding Unique Link IDs that have been selected by PMG from the lists of the ad links submitted to it by various broadcasters.

• PMG broadcasts the ad links in a fair manner, giving the users an equal opportunity to fulfil their duties, while also making sure that no single user is overburdened.

• The ad links are distributed amongst the users/receivers in a Last Recently Received manner, i.e., a user who has not received an ad link for the longest time gets the priority to receive the links first.

• Now, the receiver needs to log in to the intermediary blog directory website using their PMG credentials. Here the receiver searched for the LID that they wish to. The search result displays the actual link against this LID and the best keyword against this link. Aslo, this is when the user begins the recording of their click job using the ScreenCastify plugin of Google Chrome available freely.

• The receiver is supposed to search for that keyword in Google and look for the ad link against this keyword. Visiting the ad link and clicking on the ads should be done according to PMG’s RoE. After the ad has been visited, the user then copies the actual ad URL and pastes it back in the textbox provided for the same while capturing the video of this process.

• The receivers have to ensure that the task is completed according to PMG's RoE.

• Once done, the receiver can then select the completed tasks and send the results to the broadcaster for verification (the video proof also goes along automatically).

• The clicks made by the receiver count only after the broadcaster certifies the click as legitimate.

The Verification Process:

• After the broadcasters receive the requests for verification, they can start verifying the clicks.

• The video proof comes in handy here. Broadcasters can actually have a look at the clicks being made on the ads posted on their website.

• If the broadcaster is satisfied, they can approve the click by clicking on the "I approve" button. A confirmation notification is sent to the user who clicked on that link to let them know that the click made by them was legitimate and has been added to their progress card.

• Also, if satisfied, broadcasters are expected to rate the user who clicked on their link. This helps PMG to decide which users are doing a good job and deserve recognition, in the form of up gradation of the user level, or other benefits. This also develops an ecosystem of mutual respect and appreciation, and hence, an amicable ecosystem.

• As soon as the broadcaster verifies a click to be satisfactory, the video proof of the same will be deleted and be irrecoverable. So the broadcasters are advised to be very cautious when verifying the clicks.

• If not satisfied, the broadcaster may click on the "I disapprove" button which would notify the PMG admin who will take an appropriate action.
